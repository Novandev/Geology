Fracking and The Halliburton Loophole

Intro to Fracking

Hydraulic fracturing (Fracking) is a process in which shales are blasted at high pressure with a mixture of sand water and chemicals to reliase the natural gas contained within

The depth at which fracking takes places is between 1-2 miles underground to reach the shale that contains gas. If done right, this is usually well below the water table and shouldnt interfere with a municipalities ground water.

Due to the depth at which fracking occurs, as well at the chemicals add additives used by organizations who perform fracking to break down the shale. This process produces fissues in which these chemicals (as well as the natural gas released during the proccess) can leak into ground water, potentially poisoning a communities groundwater. This can also leak into tributaries, streams and other water sources

In 2005, the Energy Policy Act of 2005 was signed into law by (then) President Bush. The provisions of the bill inculded provisions to reward companies and loval governments for environmentally healthy practices...... and in the same breath exempted natural gas drilling from the Safe Drinking Water Act by letting companies disclose the chemicals used during hydraulic fracturing at their discretion.

The provision effectively side stepped the Environmental Protection Agency's (EPA) regulations. It is now commonly referred to as the Halliburton Loophole.

Halliburton's and Energy companies role in law and regulation

Former vice president Dick Cheney was greatly influential in developing the legal loopholes in the law that would become the Energy Policy Act of 2005, through the efforts of the Energy Task Force

The Energy Task Force was created by Cheney to "study" the effects of various industries on the environment, and adjust regulations according the the results that were observed.

Unfortunately, Dick Cheney was the Former CEO of Halliburton, which patented hydraulic fracturing in the 1940s, and remains one of the three largest manufacturers of fracturing fluids.

The Energy Policy Act of 2005 also gave FERC the ability to superseed both local, state and even the national government on Environmental regulations in regard to the regulation of fracked gas pipelines and infrastructure

National Environmental Policy Act of 1969 (which was an act signed in specifically after one of the great lakes caught fire due to the gas industries lack of regulation in the 60's) was also put under FERC control. At the time and presently FERC is headed by lobbyists and former heads of energy companies and due to how the laws were executed has no oversite from the regualr citizen, congress, or the standing president.

The effects of fracking on the environment and health

A Duke study examining 60 sites on the east coast concluded, “systematic evidence for methane contamination” in drinking water that housholds had access to. Personal water Wells a kilometerfrom drilling operations were contaminated by methane(natural gas) at around 17 times the rate of those farther from fracking sites. Although methane in water has not been studied closely as a health hazard, it can seep into houses and build up to explosive levels.
